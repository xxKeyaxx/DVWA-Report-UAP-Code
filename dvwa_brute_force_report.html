5. Conclusion

The low, medium, and high security levels are vulnerable to brute force attacks despite incremental protections. Only the "Impossible" level effectively mitigates the threat through account lockout. The test demonstrates that:

  • Without any protection, brute force attacks are trivially successful
  • Simple delays imposed by the system are insufficient to prevent determined attackers
  • CSRF tokens can be bypassed by extracting them programmatically
  • Account lockout mechanisms provide effective protection against brute force

6. Recommendations

  • Implement multi-factor authentication (MFA) for all accounts
  • Use strong rate limiting and IP-based blocking after failed attempts
  • Enforce complex password policies and regular password changes
  • Monitor and alert on repeated failed login attempts
  • Consider implementing CAPTCHA after a threshold of failed attempts
  • Use account lockout with exponential backoff for additional security
